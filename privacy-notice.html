Google Analytics does this by placing small text files called “cookies” on your computer or other device. Cookies collect information about the number of visitors to the websites, the pages visited and the time spent on the websites. This information is aggregated and not personally identifiable. In case you have any questions or concerns with the use of Google Analytics, you can block Google Analytics by installing a plug-in into your Browser. A plug-in for the most common Browsers can be downloaded here: http://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=de.

Intercom sets a cookie in your browser. You can block the user of Intercom in the same manner as Google Analytics.
